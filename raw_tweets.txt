As the summer ramps up and we see this kind of weather sweep around the Northern Hemisphere, I’m always keeping my eye on the power grid. Climate change is squeezing the grid from all sides, affecting both supply and demand. Heat can affect power availability, from generation to transmission infrastructure, as I covered in my latest story. But climate change is also helping push electricity use higher—and countries in Europe and around the world will need to adapt. In the US, nearly 90% of homes have air-conditioning. As they do, we should expect higher electricity demand, and stress for the grid—just as in the US.

链接: https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/06/25/1139696/ibm-unveils-sub1nm-chip/
正文: IBM has built a new prototype chip with around 100 billion transistors on an area the size of a fingernail, which is twice the density of the company’s previous state-of-the-art technology announced in 2021. The design could pave the way for faster and more energy efficient computers for years to come. For more than half a century, chipmakers have been able to make ever more powerful computers by following the key principle of Moore’s Law: Cram more transistors onto the chip. To do this, they shrank transistors—the tiny switches that perform computations—to incrementally smaller sizes. But in the last 15 years, transistors have gotten close to the point where quantum mechanics starts to interfere with their function: just a few dozen nanometers in size. They can’t get smaller. So to fit more transistors on a chip, engineers across the industry are eyeing a pivot to an approach familiar to urban planners: build up. On Thursday, IBM announced it has created a chip that uses this strategy. The new architecture, known as a nanostack, vertically stacks transistors in two layers on a silicon chip. “It’s not just an incremental step,” Jay Gambetta, the director of IBM Research, said during a press conference on Tuesday. “It’s a meaningful leap forward.” Within a decade, Gambetta expects, chips with nanostacking will be widely used in data centers, where their improved efficiency could help the facilities better manage their energy consumption. “Absolutely, it’s transformational,” says Dan Hutcheson, vice chair of TechInsights, a technology analysis company. “This puts another 10, 15 years on the roadmap.” Compared with IBM’s previous state-of-the-art architecture, the company reports, chips built with this new approach can do as much as 50% more work in the same amount of time and be up to 70% more energy efficient. 标题: Europe’s extreme heat is shutting down power plants
链接: https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/06/24/1139676/europe-heat-power-plants/
正文: Europe is in the middle of a record-breaking heat wave, and the grid is being pushed to its limits as people turn to fans and air-conditioning to try to stay cool. Some power plants won’t be online to help handle the load. On June 23, France saw its hottest day since record-keeping began in 1947. Temperatures climbed to over 44 °C (111 °F), and overnight temperatures remained unusually high. This prolonged hot weather warmed up the water in some rivers across the country, a problem for the many nuclear plants that rely on those bodies of water for cooling. One reactor has already shut down, and others are being ramped down or will see limitations later in the week. Unit two at the Golfech nuclear power plant in southern France shut down at about 11:45 p.m. on June 22 when the river used to cool the plant got too hot. The move was a precautionary measure, according to Brid Nelligan, a spokesperson for EDF, the plant’s owner and operator. The power plant takes in water from the Garonne River and then returns most of it to the river at slightly higher temperatures after using it to cool equipment. French regulations limit the temperature of that return stream, so the warm water (it was expected to reach 28 °C, or around 82 °F) forced the operator to shut down the plant. EDF, which operates France’s entire nuclear fleet, is also limiting the output of other reactors across the country—one reactor at the Nogent-sur-Seine power plant was ramped down as of Tuesday, and more will follow later in the week, Nelligan says. Extreme heat has affected France’s nuclear industry before. At least seven gigawatts’ worth of nuclear energy was forced to shut down across the country during a heat wave in July 2025, according to data from Ember Energy. That’s more than the entire grid of Ireland. This time, power plant outages and limitations aren’t expected to be drastic enough to affect the ability to meet demand in France, according to RTE, operator of the national electric grid.&nbsp

标题: AI被认为会取代工程岗位，但新数据显示工程是2025年最具韧性的岗位
链接: https://aihot.virxact.com/items/cmqsmijfz04cwslfuwufeuxwz
正文: AI 是否已经在取代工作岗位，这是一个激烈争论的话题。 根据外安置公司 Challenger, Gray &amp; Christmas 的数据，5 月份科技行业裁员人数创下多年来的单月最高纪录，而 AI 是被提及最多的原因。 理论上，软件工程是最容易受到自动化影响的专业领域，因为 AI 驱动的编码工具被迅速采用。然而，风险投资机构 SignalFire 的研究人员表示，招聘数据显示的情况并非如此。 “很多裁员给出的理由始终是 AI，具体来说，他们会针对代码提到 AI；他们会说，一个工程师可以完成过去多少个工程师的工作，” SignalFire 研究主管 Asher Bantock 表示。“但我们看到的一线情况与此有些出入。” SignalFire 的分析追踪了超过 8000 万家公司中数百万员工的职业发展轨迹，结果显示，工程类岗位是 2025 年最具韧性的职位。SignalFire 没有将重点放在裁员上——因为裁员后人们往往推迟更新自己的就业状态，因此难以追踪——而是将招聘数据作为衡量实时劳动力趋势的更准确指标。 根据 SignalFire 最新发布的“人才状况报告”，虽然大型科技公司的整体招聘人数较 2019 年水平下降了 25%，但工程类岗位的降幅要小得多，仅为 11%。 事实上，根据这份报告，在 SignalFire 归类为“科技巨头”的 12 家公司——Alphabet、Meta、Apple、Amazon、Microsoft、Netflix、NVIDIA、Tesla、Uber、Airbnb、Block 和 Stripe——2025 年所有新员工中，工程师占比达到 55%。这较 2019 年有显著增长，当时工程师仅占新招聘人数的 46%。 SignalFire 的数据显示，对工程师的持续需求在早期初创公司中更为明显，这些公司在 2025 年雇佣的工程师总数比 2019 年增加了 7%。 Bantock 认为，如果 AI 真的在取代工程人才，那么在当前科技招聘收缩的背景下，工程类招聘应该率先下降。然而，SignalFire 的数据显示，工程类岗位的员工数量增长速度超过科技行业中大多数其他职位。 尽管Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei去年曾警告，人工智能可能在五年内淘汰一半的初级白领岗位，并让失业率飙升至20%，但该公司自家经济学主管Peter McCrory在3月接受TechCrunch采访时表示，他尚未观察到AI对劳动力市场产生任何显著影响。 McCrory当时表示：“至少从失业率来看，没有出现更大的实质性差异”——无论是那些以“自动化方式”使用Claude执行“其工作中最核心任务”的人（如技术文档撰写员、数据录入员和软件工程师），还是那些对AI接触较少、需要“与真实世界进行物理互动和操作”的岗位，皆是如此。 Nvidia CEO黄仁勋更进一步，直接驳斥了AI将取代工程师的观点。他4月在斯坦福商学院的一次采访中说：“有人说AI会摧毁所有软件工程岗位。”随后他论证了相反的情况才是事实。他表示，现在Nvidia所有工程师都在使用智能体AI，“软件工程师比以往任何时候都更忙”。 黄仁勋补充道，虽然AI智能体几乎瞬间就能写代码，但它们一直在推动工程师去产生“下一个创意”。 至少就目前而言，在AI的加持下，工程领域已成为杰文斯悖论（Jevons paradox）的一个经典例证——效率提高并不会减少对资源的需求，反而会增加需求，因为工作会扩展以填满新的能力空间。正如Bantock在谈到当今的工程人才时所说：“他们突然变得高效得多，而且有做不完的工作等着他们。”
